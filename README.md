👩‍💼 HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

🧠 Project Summary

This project focuses on analyzing employee data to understand workforce trends, attrition patterns, and employee satisfaction. The dashboard provides data-driven insights to help HR teams improve retention and overall organizational performance.

---

🎯 Business Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition and its key drivers
- Understand workforce distribution across departments
- Evaluate employee satisfaction levels
- Identify factors impacting employee retention

---

🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used

- Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Data Cleaning
- Data Modeling

---

🗂️ Dataset Information

The dataset consists of 1470 employee records including:

- Employee demographics
- Department and job roles
- Salary and income details
- Attrition status
- Performance and satisfaction metrics

---

🔄 Data Preparation

- Removed missing and inconsistent values
- Created calculated columns and DAX measures
- Established relationships between multiple tables
- Transformed categorical variables for analysis

---

📊 Dashboard Overview

🔹 HR Dashboard

"HR Dashboard" (hr_dashboard.png)

This dashboard highlights:

- Total Employees: 1470
- Attrition Count: 237 employees
- Attrition Rate: 16.12%
- Average Age: 36.9 years
- Average Monthly Income: 6.5K

It also includes:

- Attrition by department, gender, and education
- Job role distribution
- Employee satisfaction levels

---

📈 Key Insights

- Overall attrition rate is 16.12%, indicating moderate employee turnover
- Research & Development department has the highest attrition (~56% of total attrition)
- Employees with low job satisfaction (1–2 rating) show higher exit rates
- Employees earning below 5K salary range are more likely to leave
- Male employees (~63%) contribute more to total attrition

---

💡 Features

- Interactive filters (Department, Gender, Education)

- Clean and user-friendly dashboard design
